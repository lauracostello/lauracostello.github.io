About

My PhD research involves using in-vivo neuroimaging techniques, specifically structural and diffusion MR imaging, to investigate the relationship between early adverse life events and psychosis by mapping the brains’ structural connectivity network.

About my PhD Research

Childhood trauma in psychosis: the effects of neuroanatomical dysconnectivity

Objectives To establish the relationship between childhood trauma and the disruption of normal brain development among individuals with psychosis and healthy controls.
