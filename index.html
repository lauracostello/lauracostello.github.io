Childhood trauma in psychosis: the effects of neuroanatomical dysconnectivity

The overarching goal of the proposed research is to establish the relationship between childhood trauma and neuroanatomical abnormalities in a large cohort of individuals with psychosis and healthy controls. Aims & Objectives To establish the relationship between: 1. Childhood trauma and neuroanatomical dysconnectivity present in psychosis relative to controls. 2. Childhood trauma and cortical and subcortical structural grey matter volumetric abnormalities present in psychosis relative to controls. 3. Childhood trauma and neuroinflammation within the brains’ white matter tracts present in psychosis relative to controls. Flat Icons.
